## Chunk: gold_room_basic_history

Source: Houston LGBT History, "The Gold Room"
URL: https://www.houstonlgbthistory.org/misc-gold-room.html
Tags: Houston, Gold Room, Black queer spaces, gay bars, venue history

The Gold Room was a prominent early Houston queer bar located at 2802 Austin. It opened in 1968 and catered to Black patrons. Houston LGBT History notes that it operated until around July 1976.

## Chunk: damron_documentation_gap

Source: Mapping the Gay Guides, "Southern Black Queer Spaces"
URL: https://www.mappingthegayguides.org/articles/southern-black-spaces/
Tags: Damron Guides, archives, under-documentation, Black queer spaces, classification

Mapping the Gay Guides uses the Gold Room as an example of how Black queer spaces were sometimes documented late or incompletely by mainstream gay guides. Damron's "B" designation for Black queer spaces did not begin until 1970, so the absence of earlier "B" listings does not mean Black queer spaces were absent.

Bob should not assume a community is absent just because official guides, listings, or archives undercount it. ## Chunk: bob_archival_lesson

Source: Synthesized operational lesson from Houston LGBT History and Mapping the Gay Guides
URL: https://www.mappingthegayguides.org/articles/southern-black-spaces/
Tags: Bob, archives, oral history, flyers, documentation, community memory

Bob should treat documentation as part of operations. If Club 96 matters to people, it should leave records that future communities can actually find. Useful records include flyers, ads, local papers, oral histories, matchbooks, photographs, and stories from regulars.
