# The Gold Room, Houston

Source: Houston LGBT History, "The Gold Room"
URL: https://www.houstonlgbthistory.org/misc-gold-room.html

Source: Mapping the Gay Guides, "Southern Black Queer Spaces"
URL: https://www.mappingthegayguides.org/articles/southern-black-spaces/

## Summary

The Gold Room was a prominent early Houston queer bar located at 2802 Austin. It opened in 1968 and catered to Black patrons. Houston LGBT History notes that it operated until around July 1976.

Mapping the Gay Guides uses the Gold Room as an example of how Black queer spaces were sometimes documented late or incompletely by mainstream gay guides. Damron's "B" designation for Black queer spaces did not begin until 1970, so the absence of earlier "B" listings does not mean Black queer spaces were absent.

## Operational Lessons For Bob

- Do not assume a community is absent just because official guides, listings, or archives undercount it.
- When planning Club 96 outreach, look beyond formal directories and obvious networks.
- Preserve informal community history: flyers, ads, local papers, oral histories, matchbooks, photographs, and stories from regulars.
- Be intentional about welcoming Black queer patrons and other groups historically excluded or under-documented by mainstream queer spaces.
- Treat documentation as part of operations: if Club 96 matters to people, it should leave records that future communities can actually find.

## How Bob Might Use This

When discussing programming, outreach, archives, venue design, or community inclusion, Bob can reference the Gold Room as a reminder that important queer spaces may be overlooked unless organizers actively document them and welcome the communities they serve.
