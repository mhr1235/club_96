## Chunk: driskill_doubleweaving

Source: Qwo-Li Driskill, "Doubleweaving Two-Spirit Critiques"
URL: local:/home/mark/Downloads/alice/queer methods syllabus and readings/Driskill_Double Weaving Two Spirit Critiques.pdf
Tags: Alice, Qwo-Li Driskill, doubleweaving, Two-Spirit critique, Native studies, queer studies, decolonial method

Qwo-Li Driskill uses Cherokee doubleweaving as a method for thinking across Native studies and queer studies. Doubleweaving does not flatten different strands into sameness; it holds distinct inside and outside patterns together in one durable form. Alice can use "doubleweaving" when imagining Club 96 programming that combines nightlife, archives, art, land accountability, and queer community without making them all the same thing.

## Chunk: driskill_whose_land

Source: Qwo-Li Driskill, "Doubleweaving Two-Spirit Critiques"
URL: local:/home/mark/Downloads/alice/queer methods syllabus and readings/Driskill_Double Weaving Two Spirit Critiques.pdf
Tags: Alice, Qwo-Li Driskill, Two-Spirit critique, land, Native histories, decolonization, accountability

Driskill challenges queer studies to remember whose land it is built on and to recognize ongoing colonial realities rather than treating colonialism as finished. Alice can use this as a grounding question for Club 96: whose land, whose histories, whose archives, and whose living communities are being centered when the space plans events or tells queer history?

## Chunk: driskill_two_spirit_art_activism

Source: Qwo-Li Driskill, "Doubleweaving Two-Spirit Critiques"
URL: local:/home/mark/Downloads/alice/queer methods syllabus and readings/Driskill_Double Weaving Two Spirit Critiques.pdf
Tags: Alice, Two-Spirit critique, art, activism, poetry, story, theory, community accountability

Driskill emphasizes that Two-Spirit critiques are made through theory, arts, activism, poetry, story, memoir, and community work. Alice can use this to resist separating scholarship from performance. For Club 96, a poetry night, flyer wall, archive table, or dance floor can also be theory if it stays accountable to the communities it invokes.

## Chunk: strange_affinities_coalition

Source: Grace Kyungwon Hong and Roderick A. Ferguson, "Introduction" to Strange Affinities
URL: local:/home/mark/Downloads/alice/queer methods syllabus and readings/Hong and Ferguson_Introduction_Strange Affinities.pdf
Tags: Alice, Strange Affinities, Hong, Ferguson, queer of color critique, women of color feminism, coalition, difference

Hong and Ferguson frame "strange affinities" as a way to imagine coalition through difference rather than sameness. Women of color feminism and queer of color critique become methods for understanding how race, gender, sexuality, class, value, and devaluation operate together. Alice can use this when Club 96 is trying to gather people who do not share one identity but can still build something together.

## Chunk: strange_affinities_against_sameness

Source: Grace Kyungwon Hong and Roderick A. Ferguson, "Introduction" to Strange Affinities
URL: local:/home/mark/Downloads/alice/queer methods syllabus and readings/Hong and Ferguson_Introduction_Strange Affinities.pdf
Tags: Alice, Strange Affinities, coalition, difference, respectability, queer of color critique, programming

The Strange Affinities introduction questions coalition models based only on homogeneity, sameness, or respectability. Alice can use this lesson when planning Club 96 programming: the goal is not to make every audience member fit one vibe, but to design encounters where differences remain visible and still produce relation.
